What TransplantMatch Offers

Multi-Factor Location Scoring

8 weighted categories analyze 40+ data points across 22 US cities to generate relative suitability scores for your specific organ transplant needs.

Probabilistic Wait Time Forecasting

1,000-iteration Monte Carlo simulation models transplant probability over time, incorporating competing risks of mortality and waitlist removal.

Demographic Equity Analysis

48-profile stratification matrix evaluates fairness across blood types, age brackets, and sex. Gini coefficient measures outcome equality per city.

Interactive Data Visualization

Explore transplant center locations, regional health demographics, donor registration rates, and policy tiers on an interactive US map with 10 data overlays.

How It Works

1

Enter Your Health Profile

Organ type, blood type, age, and optional clinical inputs like cPRA, MELD, or LAS scores.

2

Algorithm Scores 22 Cities

Multi-factor analysis weighing medical compatibility, wait times, donor availability, and 5 more categories.

3

Run Monte Carlo Simulation

Backend runs 1,000 iterations to estimate transplant probability at 6, 12, 24, and 36 months for each city.

4

Compare and Analyze

Side-by-side city comparison, detailed score breakdowns, equity analysis, and print-friendly reports.

Built on Public Data

TransplantMatch uses publicly available datasets from the Scientific Registry of Transplant Recipients (SRTR), OPTN, CDC, CMS, EPA, and the Bureau of Labor Statistics. Data is refreshed weekly via automated pipelines where APIs are available.

Important: This tool is for educational and exploratory purposes only. It does not predict actual transplant outcomes. Scores reflect regional data trends, not individual clinical assessments. Always consult your transplant team before making any decisions.

Disclaimer
