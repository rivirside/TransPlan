TransplantMatch: Transplant Location Analysis Tool

Compare 22 US cities across 8 scoring categories, run Monte Carlo simulations, and analyze demographic equity to explore transplant center locations.

» Launch Simulator | Features | How It Works | Documentation

Features

CapabilityDescription
Multi-Factor Scoring 8 weighted categories analyze 40+ data points across 22 US cities to generate relative suitability scores for your specific organ transplant needs.
Wait Time Forecasting 1,000-iteration Monte Carlo simulation models transplant probability over time, with competing risks of mortality and waitlist removal.
Equity Analysis 48-profile demographic matrix evaluates fairness across blood types, age brackets, and sex. Gini coefficient measures outcome equality per city.
Interactive Map Explore transplant center locations, regional health demographics, donor registration rates, and policy tiers with 10 data overlays.
What-If Scenarios Adjust donor rate and wait time multipliers to see how policy changes would affect transplant probabilities via real-time re-simulation.
Export & Reports Download results as PDF, CSV, or JSON. Export charts as PNG images. Share results via URL parameters.

How It Works

  1. Enter Your Health Profile — Organ type, blood type, age, and optional clinical inputs (cPRA, MELD, LAS).
  2. Algorithm Scores 22 Cities — Multi-factor analysis across medical compatibility, wait times, donor availability, and 5 more categories.
  3. Run Monte Carlo Simulation — 1,000 iterations estimate transplant probability at 6, 12, 24, and 36 months per city.
  4. Compare and Analyze — Side-by-side comparison, detailed breakdowns, equity analysis, and print-friendly reports.

Data Sources

TransplantMatch uses publicly available datasets from SRTR, OPTN, CDC, CMS, EPA, and the Bureau of Labor Statistics. Data is refreshed weekly via automated pipelines where APIs are available.

Disclaimer: This tool is for educational and exploratory purposes only. It does not predict actual transplant outcomes. Scores reflect regional data trends, not individual clinical assessments. Always consult your transplant team before making any decisions.
